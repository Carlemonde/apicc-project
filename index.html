APICC - Adapting Precision Irrigation to Climate Change

Project Overview

LOCATION Portugal
ORCHARD Olea Europaea
PHASE Implementation
PROGRESS 60%

Efficient water use in agriculture is a major challenge under climate change, especially due to increasing water scarcity and irregular precipitation patterns. Improving irrigation accuracy requires better estimation of crop water needs and the use of real-time information to quickly adapt to changing environmental conditions. Our project is focused on contributing to irrigation technologies that can be easily transferred and adapted across regions, while accounting for different socio-economic contexts. These solutions should be low-cost, simple to reproduce, and easy to maintain.

Digital tools have significantly improved irrigation, and commercial solutions already exist. However, high costs and limited technical support mean digital agriculture remains accessible only to a small group of farmers. Introducing SOFIS (Smart Orchard Fertigation System), a low-cost intelligent system designed to control water use in orchards and vineyards. The system combines hardware and software built with low-cost approaches and aims to enable machine learning for real-time automated data analysis and irrigation control.

Core Research Pillars

The project is built upon three fundamental technological and scientific domains.

nature

Plant Water Requirements

Inferring plant water requirements via the estimation of tree transpiration based on field data.

sensors

Low-cost Sensors

Democratizing high-tech agriculture with affordable and accurate IoT hardware solutions.

database

Data-driven Irrigation

Sensor data analysis using data mining techniques within a knowledge discovery framework.
